Ginger

Planting and growing ginger

Prepping

1. Buy a chunk of ginger from any grocer.
2. Soak the ginger in water overnight to flush out any growth inhibitors.
3. Ginger likes loose and loomy soil because this prevents waterlogging. It needs 2-5 hours of direct sunlight in warm, humid climates.

Harvesting

1. Ginger should grow to roughly 2-3 feet tall. The best time to harvest is 8-10 months.

How to keep alive for longer

1. Keep the ginger in the freezer. Take it out and break off a chunk when you need to use it.

Planting

1. Cut the ginger up, but make sure each chunk has at least one node and is decently sized.
1a. If you cut it up, give it a few days for the wound to scab before planting.
2. Soak the ginger in water overnight to flush out any growth inhibitors.
3. Ginger likes loose and loomy soil because this prevents waterlogging. It needs 2-5 hours of direct sunlight in warm, humid climates. Plant it in soil roughly 3-4 inches deep, 6-8 inches apart.
4. Apply mulch on the surface to keep out weeds and contain moisture.
5. Ginger grows in conditions warmer than 50F.
6. Fertilize with a slow-releasing fertilizer while they are still growing. Then you can switch to liquid fertilizer once they're grown.
7. Water frequently. The soil should never be dry.
