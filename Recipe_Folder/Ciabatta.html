Ciabatta

Airy Italian Bread

Biga

1 cup bread flour
1/8 tsp yeast
1/2 room temp water

Dough

1 tsp yeast
1 cup warm water
2.5 cups bread flour
2 tsp salt
Olive Oil

Prepare the biga

1. Mix biga ingredients and let rest for 24 hours. Don't overfold. It should be able to form a ball, but should still be a little sticky.

Making the dough

1. Mix the yeast and warm water. Cover and let sit for 5-10 minutes to activate the yeast.
2. Add the biga, flour, and salt and mix until the dough is wet and sticky, but uniform. Add olive oil near the end to prevent it from sticking to your hands.
3. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes
4. Stretch and Fold: Pick up the dough from the top and fold towards the bottom of the dough. Turn 90 degrees and repeat 8 times. Cover and rest again for 30 minutes.
5. Repeat the Stretch and Fold step 4 more times, resting 30 minutes between each time. Let the dough rest for another 24 hours.
6. Generously flour the baking sheet. Spread the dough out on the baking sheet and divide into multiple rectangular loaves. Flour the surface of the loaves. Don't put too much pressure on the dough or you'll deflat it.
7. Bake at 450F for 20 minutes or until the rolls are golden brown.
8. Allow the ciabatta to cook in the residue heat to retain it's shape before removing from oven.
