Japanese Cheesecake

Cheesecake

Batter

1 bar (8oz) Cream Cheese
5 Egg Yolks
1/4 cup Granulated Sugar
4 tbsp Butter
1/2 cup Milk
2 tbsp Cornstarch
1/4 cup Rice Flour

Meringue

5 Egg Whites
1/4 tsp Lime Juice
1/4 cup Granulated Sugar

Making the Batter

1. Separate the 5 eggs into whites and yolks.
2. Melt butter in a pot on low heat to prevent the butter from burning.
3. Once that's melted, add the cream cheese and sugar and cook on low heat until homogenous.
3. Remove from the heat and add the egg yolks to the batter one at a time. Continue mixing until homogenous.
4. Add corn starch and all-purpose flour. Mix until the lumps disappear and the batter becomes smooth.
5. Add milk and mix. This batter should be smooth and liquidy.

Making the Meringue

1. Start whisking the egg whites together. Use a large bowl as this will grow in size as air is beat into it. Tilt the bowl to beat the egg whites faster.
2. When you start seeing egg foam, add the lime juice.
3. When the beaten egg whites start becoming white and foamy and soft peaks form, slowly add the sugar.
4. Continue whisking until stiff peaks start forming.

Combining the Meringue and Batter

1. Once you get stiff peaks, mix 1/3 of the meringue into the batter and fold until homogenous.
2. Pour the rest of the batter into the remaining meringue and fold until homogenous.
3. If you overmix, the cake will not rise. If you undermix, the cake will not be homogenous.

Baking the cake

1. Line the ceramic baking dish with parchment paper.
2. Pour the batter into the baking dish.
3. Prepare a bain-marie by putting water in a deep pan.
4. Bake the dish at 315F for 20 minutes.
5. Once it rises, lower the heat to 270F and open the oven door for 10 seconds to drop the heat quicker. If the heat is too high, the cake might crack.
6. Bake for 1 hour.
7. Allow the cake to rest in the oven for 10 more minutes after baking. This allows the cake to cool gradually and therefore prevents it from deflating.
8. Stick a wooden stick into the cake and check if the cake is fully cooked. If it isn't fully cooked, bake the cake at 320F for 5-10 more minutes.
9. Carefully remove the ceramic baking dish from the oven. It will be very hot.

Notes

03/09/2026: My cheesecake was undercooked; I did not test it using the toothpick test. It did not hold its shape and was still watery. I think my oven is really bad at retaining heat. I shouldn't use it again. I didn't beat the meringue long enough, I only beat it for 1.5 hours for peaks to form, but not solidify. I didn't use a large enough bowl at the start, so I had to upsize to multiple bowl. One of my egg yolks broke and I got some egg white in the batter. The surface of the cheesecake didn't brown; my oven doesn't have a heating element at the top of the oven, which is also probably why I need to use my toaster oven instead. Next time, beat the meringue more efficiently and cook it in my toaster oven. And test to make sure it's cooked throughly.
03/11/2026: I baked it at 320F for 20 minutes and 285F for 30 minutes in my toaster oven and it didn't cook through. I will increase the cooking time, but I overcooked it this time to compensate. As a result, the cheesecake was moist and cooked, but tasted strongly of egg at the start. I might reduce the amount of egg yolks or add vanilla extract. I wasn't able to beat the meringue into hard peaks and there were some egg whites in liquid form. I got rid of the egg whites instead of pouring them in, which might've helped reduce the eggy taste.
03/11/2026: The electric mixer made the meringue so easy. It was easier to beat the meringue if you tilt the bowl at an angle. It took me 20 minutes instead of 1.5 hours, but I don't think I added the lime and sugar at the right times because I still couldn't achieve hard peaks. I need to fix that next time. I burned the butter while making the batter, which created dirty bits that ended up in my batter. Next time, melt on low heat. I forgot to add the flour and only remembered after it baked for 5 minutes. I forgot the milk. I had to mix it in with the meringue, which did not affect the taste because I acted quickly enough. Despite no milk, it still tasted very good. It did not taste eggy, the top browned, but did not crack, the cake rose well, but still had a sugary aftertaste, so I like this new baking time.
