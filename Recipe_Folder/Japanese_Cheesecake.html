Japanese Cheesecake

Cheesecake

Batter

1 bar (8oz) Cream Cheese
5 Egg Yolks
1/4 cup Granulated Sugar
4 tbsp Butter
1/2 cup Milk
2 tbsp Cornstarch
1/4 cup Rice Flour

Meringue

5 Egg Whites
1/4 tsp Lime Juice
1/4 cup Granulated Sugar

Making the Batter

1. Separate the 5 eggs into whites and yolks.
2. Mix the butter, cream cheese, and sugar in a pot on low heat until homogenous.
3. Remove from the heat and add the egg yolks to the batter one at a time. Continue mixing until homogenous.
4. Add corn starch and all-purpose flour. Mix until the lumps disappear and the batter becomes smooth.
5. Add milk and mix. This batter should be smooth and liquidy.

Making the Meringue

1. Start whisking the egg whites together.
2. When you start seeing egg foam, add the lime juice.
3. When the beaten egg whites start becoming white and foamy, slowly add the sugar.
4. Continue whisking until stiff peaks start forming.

Combining the Meringue and Batter

1. Once you get stiff peaks, mix 1/3 of the meringue into the batter and mix until homogenous.
2. Pour the rest of the batter into the remaining meringue and mix until homogenous.
3. If you overmix, the cake will not rise. If you undermix, the cake will not be homogenous.

Baking the cake

1. Line the ceramic baking dish with parchment paper.
2. Pour the batter into the baking dish.
3. Heat the oven to 330F and prepare a bain-marie by putting water in a deep pan.
4. Bake the dish for 25 minutes. If my cake isn't rising, I need to raise the temperature.
5. Once it rises, lower the heat to 245F and open the oven door for 10 seconds to drop the heat quicker. If the heat is too high, the cake might crack.
6. Bake for 45 more minutes.
7. Allow the cake to rest in the oven for 10 more minutes after baking.
8. Carefully remove the ceramic baking dish from the oven. It will be very hot.
