Hokkaido Milk Bread

Chinese milk bread

Tangzhong Ingredients

1/3 Bread Flour
1 cup water

Dough Ingredients

5 cups Bread Flour
4 tsp Yeast
1 tsp Kosher Salt
1/3 cup sugar
2 eggs
1 cup milk
6 tbsp butter

Making the tangzhong

1. Whisk the water and flour together over heat until the flour dissolves.
2. You should be able to draw lines in the tangzhong when it's ready.
3. Pour the tangzhong out to cool and set it aside.

Making the dough

1. Combine the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast in a bowl and mix.
2. Make a well in the center and add the milk, eggs, and tangzhong.
3. Mix everything until the dough comes together. Then add the butter and keep mixing.
4. Keep kneading until the dough turns elastic (30 min)
5. Form into a ball and place in a warm area for an hour to proof.

Making the topping

1. After proofing, divide the dough into four equal portions and roll them into a ball.
2. Let these balls proof a little longer.
3. Take a rolling pin and flatten the balls into an oval.
4. To increase the fluffiness of the bread, take one of the oval and fold it to the middle. Then fold the other end to the middle. Flip the dough over and flatten it. Repeat a few more times.
5. Flatten into an oval for the final time and roll it up. Place the rolls next to each other to form multiple buns.
6. Beat an egg and brush it over the top of the bread to give it a glossy finish.
7. Bake at 325F for 30 minutes.
